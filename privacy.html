01

Scope of this Privacy Policy

Where this policy applies

This Privacy Policy applies when you visit, register for or use Zella’s website, dashboard, AI tools, portfolio features, market analysis services, alerts, account services and other related features.

In this policy, “Zella,” “we,” “us” and “our” refer to the person or legal entity responsible for operating the Zella platform. “Personal information” means information that identifies, relates to or can reasonably be linked to an individual.

This policy does not control independent websites, exchanges, payment providers, social networks or other external services. Those providers have their own privacy practices.

02

Information we may collect

Categories of personal data

The information collected depends on the features you use and the information you choose to provide.

Account information Name, email address, display name, user identifier, profile image, membership plan and login provider.
User content Watchlists, portfolio entries, journal notes, preferences, alerts, saved analysis and information submitted to Zella.
Technical information Browser type, device type, operating system, language, approximate location, network information and error records.
Usage information Pages viewed, features used, actions taken, session activity, performance records and interaction data.
Market preferences Selected assets, timeframes, risk settings, notification preferences and analysis configurations.
Transaction information Subscription status, payment references and limited transaction records where paid services become available.
Zella should not request your cryptocurrency seed phrase, private key, complete exchange API secret or account password. Do not submit those credentials through forms, chat or support channels.
03

How information is collected

Sources of personal data

Information may be collected:

  • Directly from you when you register, complete forms, configure tools, contact support or submit content.
  • From authentication providers when you use Google Sign-In or another supported login method.
  • Automatically from your device when the Platform records security, diagnostic or usage information.
  • From connected services when you authorize Zella to receive information from an exchange, wallet or other provider.
  • From public and licensed market sources for asset prices, market statistics and blockchain information that may not identify you.

You are not required to provide every category of information. However, certain information is necessary to create an account, protect the Platform or provide a requested feature.

04

How we use information

Purposes of processing

Zella may use personal information to:

  • Create, authenticate and maintain user accounts.
  • Provide dashboards, portfolio tools, market alerts, journals, AI responses and other requested services.
  • Personalize greetings, watchlists, settings and Platform experiences.
  • Save account preferences and synchronize information between supported devices.
  • Detect fraud, abuse, unauthorized access and security incidents.
  • Diagnose technical problems, monitor availability and improve Platform performance.
  • Communicate important account, security, service and policy information.
  • Manage subscriptions, payment status and customer support.
  • Comply with applicable laws, court orders and lawful regulatory requirements.
  • Develop and improve Zella’s features, interfaces and artificial intelligence systems.
Zella does not sell cryptocurrency seed phrases, private keys or passwords. Users should never submit those credentials to the Platform.
05

Legal grounds for processing

Why processing may be permitted

Depending on the information and circumstances, Zella may process personal information on one or more of the following grounds:

  • Contract: processing needed to create an account or provide services requested by you.
  • Consent: processing you have freely agreed to, where consent is an appropriate basis.
  • Legitimate interests: reasonable interests such as security, fraud prevention, service improvement and protecting the Platform.
  • Legal obligations: processing required to comply with applicable law or lawful official requests.
  • Protection of vital interests: processing that may be necessary to protect an individual from serious harm.

Where Zella relies on consent, you may withdraw that consent for future processing. Withdrawal does not automatically make earlier lawful processing invalid.

06

Firebase and Google services

Authentication and cloud infrastructure

Zella uses Google Firebase services for account authentication and may use Cloud Firestore to store user profile information and application data.

When you create an account with an email address and password, Firebase Authentication processes information required to create and manage the authentication account.

When you use Google Sign-In, Google may provide Zella with information such as:

  • Your Google account display name.
  • Your email address.
  • Your Google user identifier.
  • Your profile image, where available.
  • Authentication tokens needed to complete the login session.

Zella does not receive your Google account password. Google’s own privacy terms apply to information processed independently by Google.

Firebase Authentication may process authentication information outside Nigeria, including in the United States. International processing may therefore apply when you create or access an account.
07

Cookies and local device storage

Information stored in your browser

Zella may use browser storage technologies, including local storage, session storage and authentication storage, to keep the Platform working.

The current Zella authentication flow may store information such as:

  • A Firebase authentication token.
  • A user record containing an account identifier, email address, display name and profile image URL.
  • Your Zella membership level.
  • Temporary splash-screen, AI initialization and market connection status.
  • Interface settings and selected preferences.

Local storage remains on your device until it is deleted by the Platform, removed through your browser settings or cleared when you sign out, depending on implementation.

Blocking required storage may prevent account login, saved settings or other important features from working correctly.

08

When information may be shared

Service providers and lawful disclosure

Zella may share limited personal information where reasonably necessary with:

  • Cloud and authentication providers that host, secure or operate parts of the Platform.
  • Payment providers where subscriptions or paid services are enabled.
  • Market-data and exchange providers when needed to provide a feature you requested.
  • Technical contractors working under appropriate confidentiality and data-protection obligations.
  • Professional advisers such as lawyers, accountants, auditors or security consultants.
  • Authorities where disclosure is required by law, court order or a valid official request.
  • A successor organization in connection with a lawful merger, restructuring, financing or transfer of the Platform.

Service providers should only receive information reasonably necessary for their assigned role and remain subject to their contractual and legal responsibilities.

09

Exchange and wallet integrations

Connected financial services

Where Zella enables exchange or wallet integrations, you may choose to authorize access to limited account or trading information.

Depending on the integration, Zella may process:

  • Exchange account identifiers.
  • Asset balances and portfolio positions.
  • Order, trade and transaction history.
  • Trading permissions and connection status.
  • Public wallet addresses and public blockchain activity.
Where possible, exchange API keys should use the minimum required permissions. Withdrawal permissions should not be enabled when an integration only needs portfolio or trading access.

Exchange and wallet providers independently process information under their own privacy policies. Removing a connection stops future access but may not automatically erase information already lawfully retained by Zella or the external provider.

10

International data transfers

Processing outside your country

Zella’s service providers, cloud systems and technical infrastructure may operate in countries outside Nigeria or outside your country of residence.

Privacy protections may differ between countries. Where required, Zella should use appropriate contractual, organizational or legal safeguards for international transfers.

By using international services such as Firebase or Google Sign-In, information required for those services may be processed in the locations where those providers operate.

11

Data retention

How long information may be kept

Zella should retain personal information only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, including:

  • Maintaining an active user account.
  • Providing requested services and saved content.
  • Preventing fraud and investigating security incidents.
  • Resolving disputes and enforcing agreements.
  • Meeting tax, accounting or other legal obligations.
  • Maintaining necessary backup and audit records.

Retention periods may differ according to the information type, sensitivity, legal requirements and whether an account remains active.

When information is no longer required, it should be deleted, anonymized or securely isolated, subject to reasonable backup and legal-retention periods.

12

Data security

Protecting personal information

Zella uses or intends to use reasonable technical and organizational safeguards designed to protect personal information. These may include:

  • Firebase Authentication for account access.
  • Encrypted HTTPS connections.
  • Firestore Security Rules and access controls.
  • Authentication-token validation.
  • Restricted administrative access.
  • Monitoring, backups and error logging.
  • Security reviews and software updates.

No internet-based system can guarantee complete security. Device compromise, weak passwords, phishing, third-party failures or unknown vulnerabilities may still create risks.

Use a unique password, protect your email account, avoid sharing login tokens and report suspected unauthorized access promptly.
13

Your privacy rights

Access and control

Subject to applicable law and relevant exceptions, you may have the right to:

Request access Ask whether Zella processes your personal information and request a copy of eligible information.
Request correction Ask Zella to correct inaccurate or incomplete personal information.
Request deletion Ask for eligible personal information or your account to be deleted.
Restrict processing Request that certain processing be limited while an issue is reviewed.
Object to processing Object to eligible processing based on legitimate interests or direct marketing.
Withdraw consent Withdraw consent where consent is the legal ground for future processing.
Request portability Request eligible information in a structured, machine-readable format.
Submit a complaint Raise a concern with Zella or an appropriate data-protection authority.

Zella may need to verify your identity before completing a privacy request. A request may be limited or refused where the law permits, including where it would affect another person’s rights or conflict with a legal obligation.

You may also clear local browser information through your browser settings. Clearing local storage may sign you out or remove saved preferences from that device.

14

Children and minors

Age-related protection

Zella’s live trading, exchange integration, subscription and financial-account features are intended for adults who are legally permitted to use them.

A person under 18 should only use suitable educational or demonstration features with the knowledge and supervision of a parent or legal guardian.

Minors should not connect an exchange account, provide payment information, execute live trades or submit sensitive financial credentials through Zella.

Where Zella learns that personal information was collected from a child without appropriate authorization, Zella should take reasonable steps to restrict or delete that information where required.
